'Para-phonic Poly-diso' is a graphic score that invites visitors of Paradiso to participate in a digital polyphonic choir.

In 11th century Italy, the music theorist Guido of Arezzo developed an ascending scale consisting of six-notes: ut, re, mi, fa, sol and la. A 7th note - 'si' or ‘ti’ - was added later. This scale forms the starting point for 'Do-Re-Mi' and solfège, a system used in music theory.

The Small Museum cabinet, which the church used in the old days for public announcements, will be transformed into a local wifi hotspot to stream a multi vocal 'Pa-Ra-Di-So Rapsodia’. While waiting on the Paradiso doorsteps, visitors can connect to the score through their phones and by doing so make the algorithmic choir composition audible.

For this multi vocal composition Remco van Bladel collaborated with Lætitia Sadier (Stereolab, Lætitia Sadier Source Ensemble).
